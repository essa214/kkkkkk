Abu Simbel Temples

The temples of Abu Simbel are considered one of the most important tourist attractions in Aswan, and the temples are currently located west of Lake Nasser, the largest artificial lake in the world, but that was not the original location of the temples, as the waters of the Great Nile River flooded that site after diverting the course of the river during the construction of the High Dam. UNESCO, with the support of the countries of the world, dismantled these temples and moved them towards the west to their current location. These temples were built by King Ramesses II in 1244 BC. The great temple is dedicated to him. It is topped by 4 huge statues of the king, and at his feet there are small statues representing the king’s sons and his wife. And his mother. The temple witnesses two important events that are repeated every year and attract thousands of tourists to watch the sun perpendicular to the face of the king’s statue in the Holy of Holies, which are the king’s birthday and the day of his coronation on the throne.

Temple of Philae, Aglika Island

The Temple of Philae is one of the most beautiful and important tourist attractions in Aswan, and it has great popularity and attractiveness in the world. It was saved from drowning by a UNESCO initiative during the construction of the High Dam in the sixties of the last century, and was transferred from Philae Island to Gilika Island, south of Lake Nasser. The highly complex process of saving the temples took nearly 9 years, and a large number of countries in the world participated in it, and advanced technological methods were used.The construction of the temple dates back to the third century BC to the worship of the god Isis, then the Greek, Roman and Byzantine eras followed it, where each era left its own stamp on the walls of the temple, where the visitor sees many huge columns and murals with wonderful drawings. As for reaching the temple, it will be through an amazing boat trip on the Nile River, in which dozens of pictures of the charming landscapes will be taken.

Unfinished obelisk

The missing obelisk is located in the southern part of the Egyptian quarries, and it is considered one of the most important tourist attractions in Aswan, as the longest Egyptian obelisks ever stretched on the ground at the site of its creation, to be a testament to the accuracy and ability of the Egyptian manufacturer in dealing with giant granite stones, and it is likely that this The obelisk was carved during the reign of Queen Hatshepsut, as most obelisks were built in her era, but a crack appeared in the body of the obelisk, which led to the cessation of its completion, so that it would remain a trace on the ground. Visitors can see many engravings of animals and birds carved on the walls of the obelisk and take many souvenir photos.

Kom Ombo Temple

The Temple of Kom Ombo was built on the banks of the Nile River, to worship the gods Horus the Great and Sobek, and it has two entrances that are identical in shape, and inside the temple there are many halls with columns with exquisite drawings and inscriptions, and many sanctuaries that highlight the extent of the interdependence and reverence of those gods, and the location of the temple was central For a group of statues that have special sanctity for the ancient Egyptians, and next to the temple there is a museum that includes many mummified crocodiles and coffins. What distinguishes this landmark, in addition to its historical status, and represents one of the tourism attractions in Aswan as well, is the way to reach this temple, which is done by sailing on Nile cruises to reach it with all the beauty of nature and the splendor of the scenes that the tourist passes through, which is worth registering through memorial shots.

Temple of Kalabsha

The Kalabsha Temple is one of the most important Egyptian-Nubian temples, and one of the most important tourist attractions in Aswan. It was built in the early Roman era in the year 30 BC in the Ptolemaic era, with a distinctive design, to worship the god Mandolis, the Nubian sun god, and it is considered one of the tourist attractions in Aswan that It can be visited through Nile cruises. Kalabsha Temple is located on an island in the middle of Lake Nasser, next to the High Dam, and about 16 km from Aswan. The temple was threatened with sinking under the waters of the Nile due to the construction of the High Dam in Aswan, but the temple was moved to the banks of Lake Nasser, and this happened as part of the UNESCO initiative to save the monuments of Nubia in the sixties of the last century.

Monastery of St. Simeon

The Monastery of Anba Semaan is considered one of the archaeological areas in Aswan that remain among the places of tourism in Aswan that receive the attention of visitors, both Christians and Muslims, especially as it is located on the path of pilgrims who come to it seeking relief from the troubles of the trip. Excavations and documents indicate that this monastery was built in the sixth century AD, and is distinguished by its unique design and its ancient role in the region by being a vivid image of brotherhood between the Christian and Islamic religions.

The monastery is located on a high hill close to the tomb of the Aga Khan, and is surrounded by the desert that extends from all sides, while the eastern side overlooks the great Nile River. The monastery took its name in relation to Anba Semaan, one of the founders of the monastery, while some call it the “Monastery of Anba Hadra” in relation to one of the prominent monks of the monastery.

Sehel Island

Suhail Island, located in the south of Aswan Governorate, is considered among the places of tourism in Aswan, as it is the title of beauty and antiquity when the charming beauty of nature meets the mystery of the past surrounded by curiosity and passion, which attracts lovers of Egypt to discover more secrets that baffle the world so far, and tempts to take memorial photos. Among the wonderful features of Suhail Island is the Nubian village, which dates back to ancient Egypt, and was a source of granite that was used in the construction of temples and obelisks. Among the most important monuments in Aswan, located on the island, is the famine painting, which is a huge mural inscribed on it in hieroglyphics, the story of the country's exposure to drought and famine due to the failure of the Nile flood, which was considered anger from the gods. King Djoser appears in the painting as he offers offerings to the god Khnum.

The plants island

There is no doubt that the island of plants is one of the most wonderful recreational places in Aswan, and it is a nature reserve built on an area of ​​​​17 acres divided into seven different plant areas, in each section of which there is a group of rare and perennial plants, where the appropriate atmosphere has been created for their growth in greenhouses and plastic greenhouses, It includes 380 species of various plants, containing a group of tropical fruits and trees, medicinal and aromatic plants, ornamental plants, wood and spice trees. One of the most enjoyable activities that attract tourists is riding a sailboat on a wonderful Nile cruise to reach the island, enjoying the most wonderful landscapes, palm trees that relax their branches on the waters of the Nile in a stunning view, and the reflection of the sun on the surface of the river that takes you to a world of magic and imagination. On the island, you will enjoy roaming the paths prepared for walking freely to discover the island, and see the rare flowers and plants, and the colorful and exotic birds.

Aswan Corniche

Certainly, you will enjoy a lot walking along the Aswan Corniche, and the best time for that is sunset or sunrise time, to watch the sunset or sunrise over the Great Nile and over the landmarks of Aswan and its desert in a wonderful and irresistible view.

If you do not want to walk along the Aswan Corniche, do not miss this view and watch it from one of the restaurants or cafes scattered on the Corniche, or in the hotel where you are staying, or you can take a picnic in one of the boats spread along the Aswan Corniche to watch the sunset in the waters of the Nile River This is much nicer.

Nile Museum

The Nile River represents the lifeblood of Egypt, and the Greek historian “Herodotus” was right when he said that “Egypt is the gift of the Nile.” Without the immortal river, Egypt would have been a barren desert with no crops or water. , a museum of its own, telling the history of ancient civilizations that lived on its banks, and the stories of the neighbors of the Nile Basin countries who linked the bonds of friendship and love between them and Egypt, which made the Nile Museum among the tourist attractions in Aswan that attract tourists from all countries of the world, especially from the sister countries of the Nile Basin.

The museum collects many traces of the ancient life of the Nile Basin countries, with indicative signs that tell a lot about the journey of the immortal river from its source in the Plateau of Lakes and the Plateau of Abyssinia (Ethiopia now) until its surplus water pours into the Mediterranean Sea. The visitor also finds documentation of the water projects that took place on the course of the river. From the early ages until the modern era, the curators of the exhibition did not neglect to add the artistic touches of the great artists of Egypt, as well as adding the ancient murals that show the ancient Egyptian farmer and his work in the stages of planting and harvesting.

Elephantine island

Elephantine Island is considered one of the most beautiful tourist attractions in Aswan, and it also has historical and strategic importance, as it was the most important border fortification of the country, and as it was the link between Egypt and its neighbor in Sudan, and an important commercial center. Its name goes back to the ancient Egyptian language (Abu), which means elephant, either because it was a center for the ivory trade, or because the island takes the shape of an elephant tusk, as some suggest. The island had a deity, the god "Khnum" in the shape of a ram's head.The tourist enjoys visiting the temples of Thutmose III of the Old Kingdom, the Temple of Khnum, Avonis III, and the Greco-Roman cemetery. As for the rest of the antiquities on the island, several articles will not suffice us to talk about them.

FlNubian Village

The Nubian village was able to be a factor attracting tourism in Aswan through its distinction in presenting a vivid and realistic picture of Nubian life, as the Nubian civilization often differed from the ancient Egyptian civilization in the way of life and the distinctive character in clothing and food, the design and decoration of houses, and the use of materials of the surrounding environment in building their homes. To achieve complete harmony with it, and to provide a healthy, refreshing, self-conditioned atmosphere away from the sweltering heat that is the character of the country. These simple things will attract you greatly, and you will be happy to spend a wonderful time in the company of a hospitable and jovial people who are good at welcoming their guests, trying Nubian clothes, famous Nubian food, handicrafts and craftsmanship, and you will also be amazed at the cleanliness of the buildings, streets and houses where the people of Nuba depend on themselves in the cleanliness of their village, Cleanliness is a way of life for them.
